Frequently Asked Questions

How MoneyMooring works, how we’re funded, and how to get the most from our guides.

Is MoneyMooring free to use?

Yes. All guides are free to read. The site is supported by advertising, including sponsored search suggestions displayed within articles, which are always distinguishable from editorial content.

Does MoneyMooring give personal financial advice?

No. We are a publisher, not a licensed advisor, bank, lender or insurer. Our guides explain how products work in general terms; decisions specific to your situation may warrant a qualified professional.

How do you make money?

Through advertising, including sponsored search units embedded in our content. Advertisers never review, approve or influence our editorial conclusions — see our editorial policy.

Are the rates and figures in your guides current?

We cite indicative ranges rather than exact quotes, because financial product pricing changes constantly. Every article shows an 'Updated' date, and we recommend verifying current terms directly with providers before acting.

Do you rank or recommend specific banks and insurers?

We deliberately avoid sponsored rankings. Our guides teach you how to compare products yourself — the criteria, the math and the fine print — rather than pushing a particular provider.

How do you choose topics?

We prioritize the money decisions most US households actually face: where to hold savings, how to insure a car, how to exit card debt, when to refinance. Reader questions sent via our contact page also shape the pipeline.

Can I republish your articles?

You may share links and brief quotations with attribution. Wholesale reproduction requires written permission — reach us via the contact page.

How do I report an error?

Email editor@moneymooring.com. Correction reports are prioritized and material fixes are reflected in the article's updated date.
